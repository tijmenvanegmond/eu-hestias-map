# Charter of the European Hearth
### A Constitutional Charter for a Living European Union

**Twelfth Draft**

---

*From many, one. From one, many. From choice, legitimacy.*

---

## Preamble

The peoples and states of Europe,

recognising that they have already built a Union of law, rights, and shared purpose;

acknowledging that unity must now evolve into plurality of participation without fragmentation of order;

affirming that democratic voice is the dividend of shared obligation;

recognising that the Union's actions extend beyond its borders and must therefore be exercised with responsibility;

determined to create a Union that is voluntary, adaptive, and constitutionally coherent;

hereby establish this Charter as the foundation of a European Union composed of Members, Inner Union Associates, Outer Union Associates, Strategic Partners, and Hestias.

---

# Title I — Foundational Principles

## Article 1 — Equal Sovereignty
All states retain sovereignty. Differences in standing reflect voluntary integration, not hierarchy.

## Article 2 — Binding Rule Principle
No actor shall be bound without procedural voice. Voice scales with obligation and exposure.

## Article 3 — Transparency
All rights, obligations, and participations shall be codified in a public Union Register.

## Article 4 — Voluntary Union
All standings include lawful pathways to deepen, step back, or exit.

## Article 5 — Living Integration
The Charter governs process, not fixed outcomes.

## Article 6 — Emergent Centre
The Union's centre exists where integration is deepest.

## Article 7 — Many Fires
Multiple Hestias may coexist, overlap, and compete within the bounded space defined by Article 8.

## Article 8 — Assembly-Locked Domains
Cross-border foundational domains are governed exclusively at Assembly level. These include the four freedoms, core single market rules, Union citizenship, immigration as it affects free movement, and the rule of law framework.

---

# Title II — Definitions

## Article 9 — Core Terms
- **Assembly Baseline:** Universally binding rules
- **Hestia:** Voluntary domain of deeper integration
- **Standing:** A state's relationship to the Union
- **External Reach:** Effects beyond participants
- **Participation:** Legal, fiscal, and regulatory involvement
- **Fiscal Key:** The financial subscription required for voting in a Hestia
- **Modular Parliament:** The European Parliament configured per legislative file

---

# Title III — Citizenship

## Article 10 — Union Citizenship
Citizens of Members are Union citizens. Union citizenship is additional to and does not replace national citizenship.

## Article 11 — Direct Rights
Free movement throughout the Inner Union, European Parliament voting (Members), petition rights, Ombudsman access, equal treatment under Union law.

## Article 12 — Inner Union Associate Citizens
- Direct Charter rights including free movement and petition
- No vote in Assembly Baseline legislation
- Full Hestia voting weight (1.0) when their state participates via Fiscal Key
- Mandatory periodic consent mechanism every ten years or sooner

## Article 13 — Outer Union & Strategic Citizens
Rights defined through bilateral compacts, with reciprocity governing Strategic Partner relationships.

## Article 14 — Citizenship Continuity
Citizens of demoted Member states retain Union citizenship for five years from the date of demotion.

---

# Title IV — Democratic Life & The Modular Parliament

## Article 15 — The Modular Parliament
The European Parliament operates as a variable-geometry legislative chamber. Voting composition is determined per legislative file according to the categories established in this Title.

The Modular Parliament treats representation as a function of obligation rather than as a function of standing. A state bound by a rule has voice on that rule. A state not bound by a rule has no voice on that rule. The same Parliament, sitting in continuous session, reconfigures itself for each file according to who is actually obligated.

## Article 16 — Principle of Weighted Voice
Voting weight reflects three factors:

1. **Legal obligation** — whether the state is bound by the rule
2. **Fiscal contribution** — whether the state pays into the relevant framework
3. **Regulatory exposure** — whether the rule materially affects the state through spillover

Weight is calculated as a function of these three factors. The default weights specified in Article 17 reflect the standard cases. The Constitutional Convention may calibrate the formula every ten years.

## Article 17 — Weighted Franchise

### Assembly Baseline Legislation
- Members: **1.0**
- Inner Union Associates: **0.0**
- Outer Union Associates: **0.0**
- Strategic Partners: **0.0**

### Hestia Legislation

**Participating Members and Inner Union Associates** (subject to Fiscal Key compliance): **1.0**

**Non-Participating Members** (conditional on demonstrable spillover certified by the Independent Legislative Classification Body): **0.3**

**Outer Union Associates** participating through compact amendment: **0.2**

**Non-Participating Inner Union Associates**: **0.0**

**Strategic Partners**: **0.0** (cooperation through the Strategic Partnership Council, not Hestia voting)

### External Reach Legislation
All states with the right to invoke the suspensive veto under Article 26 have weighted consultation rights through the Standing Review Body, calibrated by demonstrable exposure.

## Article 18 — Certification
The Speaker of the European Parliament certifies the voting configuration for each legislative file before the file is opened for debate. Certification is subject to challenge by any participating state through expedited CJEU review.

## Article 19 — Dual Majority Safeguard
Hestia decisions require:

- A majority of weighted votes
- A majority of participating states by count

This dual requirement prevents weighted majorities from being formed by small coalitions of high-weight states without broad participant support.

---

# Title V — Standings

## Article 20 — The Five Standings

- **Members:** Full rights and obligations as constitutive states of the Union
- **Inner Union Associates:** Constitutional integration with free movement, no Assembly vote, full Hestia participation rights via Fiscal Key
- **Outer Union Associates:** Treaty-based alignment with the Union, sectoral participation, gravitational orientation
- **Strategic Partners:** Reciprocal cooperation between sovereign peers
- **Hestias:** Domain-specific integration crossing the standing distinctions

## Article 21 — Standing Compacts
Each non-Member standing is governed by a bilateral compact reviewed at intervals appropriate to its scope. Compacts must be approved by both the European Parliament and the relevant democratic institutions of the partner state.

---

# Title VI — Hestias & Fiscal Architecture

## Article 22 — Cross-Standing Hestia Participation

Hestias are open to participation across the Inner and Outer Union, with rights and obligations calibrated to the participant's standing.

**Members and Inner Union Associates** may fully participate as founding or joining members, subject to the Hestia's published criteria.

**Outer Union Associates** may join existing Hestias through amendment of their Association Compact, requiring approval by the European Parliament and the Assembly of Member States. Their participation rights are specified in the amended compact.

**Strategic Partners** do not participate in Hestias as such. A Strategic Partner that wishes to participate must first transition to Outer Union Associate standing under Article 32.

No state may be excluded from joining a Hestia for political reasons. Participation criteria must be objective, published, and subject to CJEU review.

## Article 23 — The Fiscal Key

Voting rights in any Hestia require financial participation through the Fiscal Key.

1. **Subscription Requirement.** No state may exercise voting rights in a Hestia without current Fiscal Key payment in good standing.

2. **Calculation.** Fiscal Key contributions are calculated based on GDP and the participating state's exposure to the Hestia's domain. Specific formulas are determined by each Hestia Compact and certified by the Independent Review Commission.

3. **Light-Out Clause.** Non-payment results in automatic suspension of voting rights, with a sixty-day cure period. Sustained non-payment beyond the cure period triggers withdrawal procedures under Article 25.

4. **Ring-Fencing.** Each Hestia's budget is isolated from other Hestias and from the Assembly budget. A state may suspend payment in one Hestia without affecting its standing in others.

## Article 24 — Anti-Dominance Rule
No single state, nor a coalition of fewer than one-third of participating states, may dominate Hestia decision-making through weighted votes alone. The Dual Majority Safeguard of Article 19 enforces this principle.

## Article 25 — Joining, Stepping Back, and Removal

Any state satisfying the published criteria of an existing Hestia has the right to apply. Applications are assessed by the Independent Review Commission.

Any participant may withdraw through formal notification and a transition period appropriate to the domain — minimum twelve months, maximum five years for currency, financial, or defence Hestias as specified in the Hestia Compact. Withdrawal carries no penalty.

A participant may be removed by a 75% supermajority of fellow participants on grounds of systematic Compact violation, with independent assessment by the Rule of Law Monitoring Body, formal hearing, and full CJEU appeal rights.

## Article 26 — Conflict Resolution Between Hestias

Conflicts between Hestia obligations are resolved through:

- **Lex specialis** — the more specific rule prevails
- **Domain primacy** — the rule from the Hestia whose primary domain encompasses the conflict prevails when specificity is unclear
- **Conflict panel** — for structural conflicts that cannot be resolved at the rule level, escalation to a Hestia Conflict Panel comprising representatives of the conflicting Hestias, the affected state, and an independent chair appointed by the CJEU

Trade-affecting conflicts are handled under expedited procedure with decisions required within ninety days. The state caught between fires is protected throughout: it may invoke conflict suspension on the contested obligation while resolution proceeds, and shall not be penalised for participation in good faith.

## Article 27 — Promotion to the Assembly Baseline

A Hestia may be promoted to the Assembly baseline through the following procedure:

1. **Parliamentary Initiative** — two-thirds supermajority of all MEPs, with sortition panel advice
2. **Co-decision** — qualified majority approval by the Assembly of Member States
3. **Override** — if the Assembly rejects, the European Parliament may override by three-quarters supermajority of all MEPs
4. **Transition** — three to seven years for non-participating Members to align
5. **The Choice** — non-aligned Members may step back to Inner Union Associate status

The Override is the Charter's federal moment. It is rare by design, costly by acknowledgement, and reversible by amendment.

The cost is named honestly: a Member that refuses the promoted domain pays in democratic voice. It loses its vote in the Assembly that promoted the domain and its representation in the Parliament that overrode the Assembly's objection. It retains constitutional partnership through Inner Union Associate status, but surrenders its seat at the table where future integration is decided. The Charter offers exit from the obligation; the exit is paid for in voice.

This price is justified because the alternative — any single Member permanently blocking deeper integration in any domain — would prevent the Union from moving when its peoples, through extraordinary supermajorities, demand movement. The Override is constrained by CJEU review for Inviolable Floor compatibility.

# Title VII — External Reach

## Article 28 — Classification
Legislation is classified by the Independent Legislative Classification Body as Assembly, Hestia, or External Reach. Legislation that is formally internal but operates as a global standard through regulatory gravity is presumed External Reach unless demonstrated otherwise.

## Article 29 — The Standing Review Body
A permanent institution responsible for handling consultation and suspensive veto procedures on External Reach legislation across all standings. Affected states across all standings — Inner Union Associates, Outer Union Associates, Strategic Partners, and non-participating Members — hold consultation rights and the right to invoke the suspensive veto.

## Article 30 — The Suspensive Veto
External Reach legislation may be paused for a six-month review period (or thirty days for trade-affecting matters) at the request of affected states. The Standing Review Body may propose amendments, exemptions, transition periods, or mitigation measures. After the review period, the legislating body may proceed with or without modification.

The Charter is honest about the cost. The suspensive veto is a deliberate constraint on the Union's regulatory autonomy. It exists because the alternative — legislating for the world without consulting the world — violates the Binding Rule Principle.

## Article 31 — Escalation
Unresolved External Reach disputes escalate first to a Joint Review Panel and then to a CJEU advisory opinion.

---

# Title VIII — Mobility Between Standings

## Article 32 — Right to Deepen
Any state may apply for a deeper standing for which it meets published criteria. Strategic Partners seeking gravitational alignment may transition to Outer Union Associate. Outer Union Associates may apply for Inner Union Associate status. Inner Union Associates may apply for Membership. Members may join Hestias.

Criteria are legal questions assessed by the Independent Review Commission, not diplomatic negotiations.

## Article 33 — Right to Step Back
Any state may voluntarily reduce its integration through transitions defined by procedure, with appropriate transition periods and no penalty.

## Article 34 — Integration Gravity Review
Inner Union Associates whose Hestia participation crosses a threshold defined by the Constitutional Convention shall trigger an automatic compact review. The review may recommend deepening the compact, addressing specific anomalies, or inviting the state to consider Membership. The reviewed state retains full sovereignty over the response. No state may be transitioned to a different standing against its will through this procedure.

## Article 35 — Demotion
A Member may be moved to Inner Union Associate status by 75% Assembly supermajority by population and state count, Parliament approval, six-month warning period, formal hearing, and full CJEU appeal rights. Demotion is reviewed automatically every two years. Citizenship continuity under Article 14 applies.

---

# Title IX — The Inviolable Floor

## Article 36 — Guaranteed Rights Across All Standings

Regardless of standing, every state and its people are guaranteed:

- The right to be heard before binding rules are finalised
- Independent dispute resolution
- Protection against unilateral alteration of terms
- Access to baseline Union programmes in science, culture, and humanitarian cooperation
- The right to exit the Union framework entirely
- Non-discrimination on grounds of standing

These rights may not be overridden by any procedure of this Charter, including the Promotion Override of Article 27.

## Article 37 — Right of Exit
Any state may withdraw from the Union framework entirely. Exit shall not invalidate treaties or obligations entered into during participation. Citizenship rights of individuals shall be subject to transitional protections of no less than five years.

---

# Title X — Crisis Clause

## Article 38 — Emergency Mode

In extreme crises threatening the security, constitutional order, or fundamental functioning of the Union, the Union may enter Emergency Mode for a strictly limited period.

**Declaration** requires a four-fifths supermajority of both the European Parliament and the Assembly of Member States, voting concurrently. Either body may end Emergency Mode unilaterally by simple majority.

**Scope** is limited to specific enumerated powers defined in the declaration. Emergency Mode does not grant general centralisation. It authorises only those specific actions necessary to address the declared crisis.

**Inviolable Floor protections under Title IX cannot be suspended** under any Emergency Mode declaration. The right of exit, the right to be heard, and the protections of citizenship continuity remain in force.

**Time limits** are concrete: an initial period of ninety days, renewable once for an additional ninety days by the same four-fifths supermajority. Total Emergency Mode duration may not exceed one hundred and eighty days without Charter amendment.

**Judicial review** by the CJEU is available throughout Emergency Mode and is expedited. The CJEU may strike down specific actions taken under Emergency powers if they exceed the declared scope or violate the Inviolable Floor.

**Post-crisis ratification** is mandatory. Every action taken under Emergency Mode requires retroactive approval by both the European Parliament and the Assembly of Member States within six months of the crisis ending. Actions not ratified are null and the affected parties have a right to remedy.

The Crisis Clause exists because constitutional systems must be able to act in genuine emergencies. The constraints exist because crisis powers are the most-abused provisions in any constitution.

# Title XI — Institutions

## Article 39 — Political Institutions

**The European Parliament** — directly elected by all Union citizens, operating as the Modular Parliament defined in Title IV.

**The Assembly of Member States** — intergovernmental body comprising representatives of all Members, co-legislating with the Parliament on Assembly Baseline matters.

**The European Commission** — executive body. The President is elected by the European Parliament from candidates proposed by European political parties.

**The Court of Justice of the European Union** — judicial authority responsible for interpretation of this Charter, review of legislation, judicial review of Independent Body decisions, review of Override decisions for Inviolable Floor compatibility, and resolution of disputes.

## Article 40 — Independent Bodies and the Triple Key System

The Union maintains four independent bodies: the Independent Review Commission, the Independent Legislative Classification Body, the Rule of Law Monitoring Body, and the Standing Review Body.

These bodies operate under the Triple Key system of accountability:

**Appointment.** Members are appointed by the concurrent agreement of three keys: a supermajority of the European Parliament, a supermajority of the Assembly of Member States, and confirmation by the Court of Justice plenary. No appointment is valid without all three keys turning.

**Terms.** Non-renewable terms of meaningful length — eight years for the Independent Review Commission, seven years for the other bodies — staggered to prevent capture by any single political moment.

**Eligibility.** No member may be a serving official of any national government, Union institution, political party, or commercial entity during tenure, with a three-year cooling-off period after.

**Removal.** Members may be removed only for cause — incapacity, gross misconduct, or eligibility breach — by a procedure requiring a higher threshold than appointment: three-quarters of the Parliament, three-quarters of the Assembly, and Court of Justice approval. Members may not be removed for the substance of their decisions.

**Transparency.** All decisions are published with full reasoning. Dissenting opinions are permitted and published alongside majority decisions. Deliberations are recorded in summary form and made public after a five-year delay.

**Review.** Decisions are reviewable by the CJEU on legal grounds. The Constitutional Convention may issue findings on systematic interpretation problems, which feed into subsequent appointments but do not overturn individual decisions.

**Reporting.** Each body publishes an annual report and a comprehensive five-year account of how its interpretations have evolved.

The Triple Key system exists because the Union's independent bodies will make politically explosive decisions: whether a state is in rule of law breach, whether a domain is Hestia-eligible, whether a rule has External Reach. The Charter cannot pretend these are merely technical determinations. It must give the bodies enough independence to make hard calls and enough accountability to remain democratically legitimate.

## Article 41 — The Constitutional Convention
A standing body comprising delegates of the European Parliament, national parliaments (two per Member, one per Inner Union Associate), citizen delegates by sortition, and representatives of each active Hestia. Meets every five years in ordinary session and in extraordinary session by petition of 30% of Member states or majority of the European Parliament.

---

# Title XII — Amendment

## Article 42 — Amendment Procedure

**Foundational provisions** (Titles I, III, IX, X, and this Article) require unanimous Member consent, supermajority of the European Parliament, and ratification by a supermajority of Inner Union Associates.

**Institutional and procedural provisions** require supermajority of the Assembly of Member States and majority of the European Parliament, with Inner Union Associate consultation.

**Technical criteria and thresholds** are amendable by the Constitutional Convention with endorsement by Assembly and Parliament.

This procedure is itself constitutionally protected.

---

# Title XIII — Entry into Force

## Article 43 — The Founding Hestia
Upon ratification, the existing acquis becomes the Founding Hestia. Existing relationships are mapped to the standings of this Charter by the Independent Review Commission within three years, respecting the choices of the affected states.

## Article 44 — Foundation Coalition
Five states constituting a viable Assembly may ratify this Charter and establish its institutions, inviting others to accede at any standing.

## Article 45 — Relationship to Existing Treaties
Upon ratification, this Charter supersedes conflicting provisions of existing EU treaties among ratifying states. Transitional protocols for non-ratifying state interdependencies shall be negotiated within two years.

*From many nations, one union was forged. From that one union, many fires now burn — each lit by choice, each tended by those who gather around it.*
