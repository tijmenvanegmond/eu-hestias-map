# Hearthwork EU Charter

### A Constitutional Charter for a Living European Union

**Fourteenth Draft**

---

*From many, one. From one, many. From choice, legitimacy.*

---

## Preamble

The peoples and states of Europe,

recognising that they have already built a Union of law, rights, and shared purpose;

acknowledging that unity must evolve into plurality of participation without fragmentation of order;

affirming that democratic voice is the dividend of shared obligation;

determined to create a Union that is **flexible, accountable, and close to its citizens**;

hereby establish this Charter as the foundation of a European Union composed of **Full Members, Inner Union Associates, Outer Union Associates, and Strategic Partners**.

---

# Title I — Foundational Principles

**Article 1 — Equal Sovereignty.** All states retain sovereignty. Differences in standing reflect voluntary integration, not hierarchy.

### Article 2: Standings of Participation

The Union recognizes five standings:

1. **Full Members**: Participate in all Hestias, except those they explicitly opt out of.
2. **Inner Union Associates**: Participate in a broad set of Hestias, with a pathway to Full Membership.
3. **Outer Union Associates**: Participate in a limited set of Hestias, with flexibility to deepen integration.
4. **Strategic Partners**: Non-EU states with a voice in Hestias with global impact (e.g., trade, climate).

# Title II — Hestias and Governance

### Article 15: Hierarchy of Rules

1. The Assembly Baseline sets minimum standards for all members.
2. Hestias may adopt stricter rules but cannot derogate from the Baseline unless explicitly permitted by a 2/3 majority of the Assembly.
3. In case of conflict, the Baseline prevails unless the Hestia explicitly derogates.

### Article 16: Hestia Templates

Each Hestia must include:

- Goals and scope.
- Funding mechanism (e.g., GDP-based contributions).
- Decision-making process (e.g., qualified majority voting).
- Entry/exit rules.

# Title III — Citizen Participation and Transparency

### Article 20: Citizen Participation

1. Each Hestia will establish a Citizen Panel of 100 randomly selected residents from participating states.
2. Panels will:
  - Review proposals before votes.
  - Publish public reports on potential impacts.

### Article 21: Transparency Tools

The Assembly will create and maintain a "My EU" portal allowing citizens to:

- Track their state's participation in Hestias.
- Simulate impacts of joining/leaving Hestias.
- Submit questions and petitions to representatives.

# Title IV — Decision-Making and Power Balance

### Article 17: Decision-Making in Hestias

1. Decisions require:
  - A majority of participating states.
  - A majority of the participating population (or GDP, where relevant).

### Article 18: Hestia Leadership

- Presidency of each Hestia rotates annually among participating states, with smaller states prioritized.

# Title V — Crisis and Emergency Measures

### Article 30: Emergency Measures

1. A limited set of pre-approved emergency measures (e.g., joint procurement, border controls) can be activated by a simple majority of the Assembly.
2. Measures expire after 90 days unless ratified.
3. A public debrief will be held within 30 days of expiration.

# Title VI — Independent Bodies

### Article 25: Independent Bodies

- Nominations for Triple Key bodies will be conducted in public hearings, with candidate Q&As livestreamed.
- Members serve one 6-year term, with a 3-year cooling-off period.

# Title VII — Pilot and Review

### Article 40: Pilot Phase

1. The Assembly will launch a pilot Hestia within 12 months of this Charter's adoption.
2. The pilot will test:
  - Opt-in/opt-out mechanisms.
  - Citizen participation tools.
  - Conflict resolution processes.

### Article 41: Simplification Review

- The Assembly will review and simplify one aspect of the Charter annually, based on citizen and member state feedback.

---

*This Charter is a living document, designed to evolve with the needs and aspirations of the peoples of Europe.*
